# One product, two surfaces

The daily check-off and its sister monthly-to-yearly maintenance app merge into a single product with two surfaces — **Core** and **Rare** — rather than staying two apps that only share styling. The two are distinguished by *posture*, not frequency: Core is the pile-up areas you've committed to a rhythm and clear the day they come due (_today_); Rare is slower upkeep done when time and energy allow, plus a few real deadlines (_when you can_). They share a kernel of language — Task, Category, Schedule, Occurrence, Done-through (the last identical in both, down to the stored `task-id → done-through` shape) — but stay two bounded contexts, because the surfaces genuinely disagree on how a Schedule places a Task in time: Core by weekday slots across a two-week parity cycle, Rare by anchor-date plus interval. One merged glossary would blur both; two peer apps would throw away the Core/Rare framing the language already implied and force every shared concern to be built twice. Code reflects this: `app.shell` is a thin responsive container hosting both surfaces, `app.shared.*` holds storage, sync, config, date and done-through primitives plus the cross-cutting view hooks, and `app.core.*` / `app.rare.*` hold each surface's store, view, schedule, and seed. Both surfaces converge on the maintenance app's cleaner state split — a pure core (`init-state`/`toggle`/`select`, plain maps in and out) wrapped by a thin atom store and a subscription hook, with sync slotting in behind `init-state` — adopting it over the check-off surface's hooks-in-core, whose business logic was tangled into React and hard to test. Rare's Schedule moves from JSON to EDN to match Core, deleting its key-normalisation step and letting one parser and one gist fetch serve both (per 0005, now a single combined gist `{:core … :rare …}` behind the existing pointer).

## Consequences

- The retired maintenance repo's history is not preserved; its source is copied in, and the repo is archived.
- "Core" and "Rare" are working names for an axis that is posture, not cadence — the glossary, not the names, carries the distinction.
- The single header and the no-surface-labels shell mean the two surfaces are told apart by form (button grid vs maintenance cards), not by signage.
